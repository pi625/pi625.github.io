This is where my mathematical writing lives — from careful, example-first
expositions to loose notes and half-finished ideas.

The through-line for much of it is the **polynomial method**: the surprising
idea that counting the roots of a well-chosen polynomial can settle questions
about sets, sums, and structure. That thread runs from Cauchy–Davenport all the
way to Alon's Combinatorial Nullstellensatz.

Longer pieces are posted on the [blog]({{ '/blog/' | relative_url }}); this page
will grow into a more organized index over time.
