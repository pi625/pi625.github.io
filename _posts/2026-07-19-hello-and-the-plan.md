Short one to open the blog.

**nullstellensatz** is my entry for the [Summer of Math Exposition](https://some.3b1b.co/).
The theme is the *polynomial method* — a handful of tricks that repeatedly turn
combinatorial questions into questions about polynomials, where they suddenly become
easy.

Here is the rough map:

1. **The mechanism.** The [Combinatorial Nullstellensatz]({% post_url 2026-07-18-combinatorial-nullstellensatz %})
   and why a single non-zero coefficient is so powerful.
2. **Sumsets.** Cauchy–Davenport and Erdős–Heilbronn, done the polynomial way.
3. **Finite fields.** Chevalley–Warning and counting solutions.
4. **Competition corner.** A few olympiad problems that fall to the same idea.

Everything is example-first: play with a small case, notice the pattern, *then*
name the theorem. If a step feels like it appeared out of nowhere, that's a bug —
tell me.

See you in the [next post]({% post_url 2026-07-18-combinatorial-nullstellensatz %}).
